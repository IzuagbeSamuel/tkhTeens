Your Responsibilities as a Christian

May 17, 2021 | Samuel Izuagbe

Every member of an organization, every human has certain responsibilities that are required to fulfill. These varies from every individual. The Kingdom of God is no exception. We all have responsibilities, obligations and duties to discharge.


  1. STUDY THE WORD OF GOD:

    2. The Word of God is the beginning for a successful Christian life. As Christians we are commanded to meditate, hear, read, studying, and apply on it day and night. Studying the Word of God discloses our inheritance as believers, It gives us confidence to share the gospel, studying the Word of God is the only way to the revelation of God and his plans for man, the Word of God discipline the believers and help them grow in faith.

    • (Ezra 7:10):

      • "For Ezra had set his heart to study the Law of the Lord, and to do it and to teach his statutes and rules in Isreal."


    • (Acts 20:32)

      • "And now, brethren, I command you to God, and to the Word of his grace, which is able to build you up, and to give you an inheritance among all them which are sanctified."


  2. PREACH/TEACH HTE WORD OF GOD:

    3. As Christians it is our mission to preach or teach the Gospel to the unbelievers. Everywhere Jesus went he taught and preached God's precious Word. This responsibility sums up our whole mission as believers, preach to the unbelievers, disciple them, and send them to the harvest field is the mission for all believers.

    • (Matthew 5:16):

      • "Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works and glorify your Father in heaven"


    • (Matthew 28:18-20):

      • And Jesus came up and spoke to them, saying, "All authority has been given to Me in heaven and on earth. Go therefore and make disciples of all the nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit, teaching them to observe all that I commanded you; and lo, I am with you always, even to the end of the age."


  3. PRAY

    4. Prayer is a way of relating: to God, to ourselves, to those around us. In opening our hearts and minds and souls to God we are challenged to grow, change, and to love. Just as we make time to spend with those we love, so in making time to pray we are seeking to grow in our relationship with the Divine. Prayer is the primary way for the believer in Jesus Christ to communicate his emotions and desires with God and to fellowship with God, it is direct address to God. It is the communication of the human soul with the Lord who created the soul.

    • (1 John 5:14)

      • "This is the confidence we have in approaching God; that is we ask anything according to his will, he hears us."


    • (Ephesians 6:18)

      • "And pray in the spirit on all occasions with all kinds of prayers and requests, With this in mind, be alert and always keep on praying for all the Lord's people"


  4. LOVE

    5. Jesus made us know what love is. By laying down His life for us, He gave us and everlasting understanding of true love. Because of God's love for the world, we understand how love is and it is also undeserving and often unrequited. We occasionally love and care for ourselves, the bible did not really tell us to love ourselves, because it is not necessary. The point is that we all take care of ourselves, put ourselves, desires and ambitions first, before God and other people. As Christians we are to share and show love to God and other people around us and be selfless.

    • (Philippians 2:3)

      • "Do nothing out of selfish ambition or vain conceit. Rather, in humility value others above yourselves"


    • (Matthew 22:39)

      • "Love your neighbor as yourself"


  5. WORSHIP

    6. Worship is a way to express honor to God through devotion, admiration, and great respect. When we tend to be living a good life, we begin to take certain things for granted, such as having a safe place to lay our head, having enough food to eat. Counting those blessings make our lives better. As Christians, we are ought to always give thanks to the LORD for all he has provided. There are several ways on how we can worship God:

    1. Thank Him in Prayer
    2. Sing Praise
    3. Share Your Story

    • (Psalms 29:2)

      • "Honor the LORD for the glory of his name. Worship the Lord in the splendor of his holiness"


    • (Psalm 95:1-6)

      • 1. Come, let us sing for joy to the Lord; let us shout aloud to the Rock of our salvation. 2. Let us come before him with thanksgiving and extol him with music and song. 3. For the Lord is the great God, the great King above all gods. 4. In his hand are the depths of the earth, and the mountain peaks belong to him. 5. The sea is his, for he made it, and his hands formed the dry land. 6. Come, let us bow down in worship, let us kneel before the Lord our Maker;


  6. CONTROLLING THE TONGUE

    7. The tongue is a small part of the body that boast of great things. It's considered as a little fire which can start up the burning of a forest. James talked about how little the tongue is but can cause a great disaster, James didn't really talk about how much words the tongue pour out but how strong our tongue to have the capacity to effect massive result. A man who has control over his tongue has massive control over his entire body. The point about the tongue being powerful doesn't really mean everything it does is positive.

    As Christians we are meant to always say the truth and keep our tongue from evil, to love the truth and always say the truth. Solomon said in the book of Proverbs vs 10 that as Christians we tend to sin when we talk too much, the point is that a wise man knows when to stop talking. It's better not to speak than to speak with regrets. A good person brings good things out of the good stored up in him, and a bad person brings out bad things stored up in him but in the day of Judgement we are all going to give account to all the careless words we have spoken.

    • (James 3:5-6)

      • " Even so the tongue is a little member, and boasteth great things. Behold, how great a matter a little fire kindleth! 6. And the tongue is a fire, a world of iniquity: so is the tongue among our members, that it defileth the whole body, and setteth on fire the course of nature; and it is set on fire of hell"


    • (Proverbs 10:19)

      • " In the multitude of words there wanteth not sin: but he that refraineth his lips is wise"
